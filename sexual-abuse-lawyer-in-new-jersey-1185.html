    Identifying the Categories of Sexual Abuse

    Sexual assault can manifest in various forms, creating a complex landscape that survivors must navigate. Physical abuse involves involuntary physical contact, which can include groping or penetration. Emotional abuse, on the other hand, often involves manipulation, coercion, or threats that instill fear and can have lasting psychological effects on victims. Additionally, psychological abuse may include derogatory comments or intimidation, which can compound the trauma experienced by survivors.

    Another significant category is online abuse, where perpetrators exploit technology to harass or exploit individuals. This can include sharing explicit images without consent or engaging in grooming behaviors over the internet. Systemic abuse occurs when an institution fails to protect individuals from harm, often seen in schools or care facilities. Each type of abuse requires a tailored approach for legal intervention and support, emphasizing the importance of understanding the specific circumstances surrounding each case.
